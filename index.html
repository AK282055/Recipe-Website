Poha

20 mins Easy

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups flattened rice (poha)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 1 medium potato, diced (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon peanuts
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse poha in a colander under running water for a few seconds. Let it sit for 5 minutes to soften.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds. Let them splutter.
  3. Add peanuts and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add chopped onions, green chili, and curry leaves. Sauté until onions turn soft.
  5. Add diced potatoes (if using) and cook until tender.
  6. Add turmeric powder, salt, and sugar, and mix well.
  7. Add the soaked poha and mix gently to coat it with the spices.
  8. Cover and cook for 2 minutes on low flame.
  9. Turn off the heat and add lemon juice.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.

Masala Dosa

45 mins Hard

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

For the Masala Filling:

  • 3 boiled potatoes, mashed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds for 6 hours. Grind to a smooth batter and ferment overnight.
  2. For masala, heat oil, add mustard seeds, curry leaves, onion, and green chili. Sauté until soft.
  3. Add turmeric, salt, and mashed potatoes. Mix well and cook for 2 minutes.
  4. Heat a pan, spread dosa batter thinly, drizzle oil, and cook until crisp.
  5. Place masala in the center, fold the dosa, and serve hot with chutney and sambar.

Idli Sambhar

40 mins Medium

Ingredients for Idli:

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1 cup urad dal
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste

Ingredients for Sambar:

  • 1/2 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 1 tablespoon sambar powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds for 6 hours. Grind to a smooth batter and ferment overnight.
  2. Pour batter into idli molds and steam for 10 minutes.
  3. For sambar, pressure cook dal with turmeric until soft.
  4. Heat oil, add mustard seeds, curry leaves, onion, tomato, and vegetables. Sauté for 5 minutes.
  5. Add cooked dal, sambar powder, salt, and simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. Serve hot idlis with sambar and chutney.

Aloo Paratha

30 mins Medium

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 boiled potatoes, mashed
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves
  • Butter or ghee for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Knead wheat flour with water into a soft dough. Let it rest for 20 minutes.
  2. Mix mashed potatoes with spices, green chili, and coriander.
  3. Roll out dough, place filling, seal edges, and roll again.
  4. Cook on a hot tawa with ghee until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Serve hot with butter, curd, or pickle.

Rajma Chawal

45 mins Medium

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup kidney beans (rajma), soaked overnight
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups cooked rice

Instructions:

  1. Pressure cook the soaked rajma until soft.
  2. Heat oil, add cumin seeds, and sauté onions.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and tomato puree, cook until oil separates.
  4. Add turmeric, salt, and garam masala. Mix well.
  5. Add cooked rajma with some water and simmer for 15 minutes.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Kadhi Pakoda

50 mins Medium

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1 cup curd (yogurt), whisked
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as required
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Mix besan, salt, and water to make a thick batter for pakoras.
  2. Heat oil and deep-fry small portions of batter until golden brown. Keep aside.
  3. In another pan, heat oil, add cumin & mustard seeds.
  4. Add onions, green chilies, turmeric, and chili powder. Sauté until golden.
  5. Mix curd with besan and water, then add to the pan.
  6. Simmer on low heat for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Add fried pakoras and cook for 5 more minutes.
  8. Garnish with coriander and serve hot.

Crispy Corn

20 mins Easy

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sweet corn kernels
  • 2 tbsp cornflour
  • 1 tbsp rice flour
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp chaat masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying
  • 1 tbsp chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 green chili (chopped, optional)
  • 1/2 lemon (for garnishing)

Instructions:

  1. Boil the sweet corn for 5 minutes and drain the excess water.
  2. In a bowl, mix cornflour, rice flour, black pepper, and salt.
  3. Add the boiled corn to this mixture and coat well.
  4. Heat oil in a pan and deep-fry the coated corn until golden and crispy.
  5. Remove excess oil using a tissue and transfer to a bowl.
  6. Sprinkle red chili powder, chaat masala, and coriander leaves.
  7. Garnish with chopped green chili and a squeeze of lemon.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy crispy, spicy corn!

Instructions:

Gajar Ka Halwa

50 mins Medium

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups grated carrots
  • 2 cups full cream milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust as per taste)
  • 1/4 cup ghee
  • 1/2 cup khoya (optional, for richness)
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 10-12 cashews
  • 10-12 almonds (sliced)
  • 10-12 raisins

Instructions:

  1. Heat ghee in a heavy-bottom pan and sauté grated carrots for 5-7 minutes.
  2. Add milk and cook on a medium flame until the milk reduces and the carrots soften.
  3. Stir occasionally to prevent burning and cook until most of the milk evaporates.
  4. Add sugar and mix well; cook for another 5 minutes.
  5. Now add khoya (if using) and stir well.
  6. Sprinkle cardamom powder and mix.
  7. In a separate pan, fry cashews, almonds, and raisins in a little ghee.
  8. Garnish the halwa with fried dry fruits.
  9. Serve warm and enjoy the rich and delicious Gajar Ka Halwa!

Kheer

40 mins Easy

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup basmati rice
  • 4 cups full cream milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust as per taste)
  • 1/4 cup chopped cashews, almonds, and pistachios
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 8-10 saffron strands (optional)
  • 1 tbsp ghee
  • 10-12 raisins

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak rice for 30 minutes.
  2. Boil milk in a heavy-bottom pan, then add soaked rice.
  3. Cook on a low flame, stirring occasionally, until the rice softens and the milk thickens.
  4. Add sugar and mix well until it dissolves completely.
  5. Add cardamom powder and saffron strands (if using) and stir.
  6. In a small pan, heat ghee and fry the dry fruits until golden.
  7. Add the fried dry fruits to the kheer and mix well.
  8. Simmer for 5 more minutes until the flavors blend.
  9. Serve warm or chilled and enjoy the delicious Kheer!

Gulab Jamun

45 mins Medium

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup khoya (mawa), grated
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/8 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp milk (as needed for kneading)
  • 2 tbsp ghee (for dough)
  • Oil or ghee (for deep frying)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/2 tsp rose water (optional)
  • Few saffron strands (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix grated khoya, all-purpose flour, and baking soda.
  2. Add ghee and knead gently with milk to form a soft, smooth dough.
  3. Divide the dough into small equal-sized balls and ensure there are no cracks.
  4. In a pan, heat oil or ghee on low flame and fry the balls until golden brown. Stir continuously for even frying.
  5. In another pan, heat water and sugar to make sugar syrup. Boil for 5 minutes.
  6. Add cardamom powder, rose water, and saffron strands (if using) to the syrup.
  7. Soak the fried Gulab Jamuns in warm sugar syrup for at least 2-3 hours.
  8. Serve warm or chilled and enjoy soft and juicy Gulab Jamuns!

Besan Ke Ladoo

40 mins Medium

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups besan (gram flour)
  • 3/4 cup ghee
  • 3/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 2 tbsp chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios)

Instructions:

  1. Heat ghee in a pan on low flame, then add besan.
  2. Roast the besan on low flame, stirring continuously until it turns golden brown and aromatic (about 15-20 minutes).
  3. Add chopped nuts and mix well.
  4. Turn off the heat and let the mixture cool slightly.
  5. Once warm, add powdered sugar and cardamom powder, and mix thoroughly.
  6. Take small portions of the mixture and shape them into round ladoos.
  7. Let them set for 10-15 minutes and store in an airtight container.
  8. Enjoy homemade besan ladoos!

Jalebi

50 mins Hard

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp corn flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • Water (as needed to make a batter)
  • 1/2 tsp saffron or food color (optional)
  • Ghee or oil (for frying)

For Sugar Syrup:

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/2 tsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp saffron strands (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, corn flour, baking soda, yogurt, and water to make a smooth batter.
  2. Cover and let the batter ferment for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  3. Heat sugar and water in a pan to make sugar syrup, add saffron, cardamom powder, and lemon juice.
  4. Stir the syrup until it reaches a sticky consistency (not too thick).
  5. Heat oil or ghee in a pan.
  6. Pour the batter into a piping bag or squeeze bottle and make spiral shapes in hot oil.
  7. Fry until golden and crisp, then remove and drain excess oil.
  8. Immediately dip the jalebis in warm sugar syrup for 30 seconds, then remove.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy crispy, sweet jalebis!

Raj Bhog

45 mins Medium

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice (mixed with 2 tbsp water)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/4 tsp saffron strands
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/2 tsp rose water (optional)
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped pistachios (for garnish)
  • A few drops yellow food color (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Boil the milk in a pan and add lemon juice while stirring gently.
  2. Once the milk curdles, strain it using a muslin cloth and rinse with cold water.
  3. Squeeze out excess water and let it hang for 30 minutes to remove moisture.
  4. Knead the chhena (paneer) into a smooth dough, then add saffron strands and food color.
  5. Divide the dough into small balls and ensure there are no cracks.
  6. In a separate pan, boil water and sugar to make a sugar syrup.
  7. Add cardamom powder and rose water to the syrup.
  8. Drop the chhena balls into the boiling syrup and cover with a lid.
  9. Cook for 15-20 minutes on medium heat until they double in size.
  10. Turn off the heat and let them soak in the syrup for 2-3 hours.
  11. Garnish with chopped pistachios and serve chilled.

Rasmalai

60 mins Hard

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice (mixed with 2 tbsp water)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/4 cup chopped pistachios & almonds
  • A few saffron strands
  • 1/2 tsp rose water (optional)
  • 500 ml milk (for rabri)
  • 1/4 cup condensed milk (optional for extra sweetness)

Instructions:

  1. Boil 1 liter milk in a pan and add lemon juice while stirring gently.
  2. Once the milk curdles, strain using a muslin cloth and rinse with cold water.
  3. Squeeze out excess water and hang for 30 minutes to remove moisture.
  4. Knead the chhena (paneer) into a smooth dough and make small balls.
  5. In a separate pan, boil water and sugar to make a sugar syrup.
  6. Add the chhena balls to the boiling syrup and cover with a lid.
  7. Cook for 15-20 minutes on medium heat until they double in size.
  8. Meanwhile, prepare rabri by boiling 500 ml milk until it thickens slightly.
  9. Add cardamom powder, saffron, chopped nuts, and condensed milk.
  10. Remove the chhena balls from syrup, squeeze gently, and add to the rabri.
  11. Chill for a few hours before serving. Garnish with more nuts.

Ice Cream

4 hours Easy

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream (chilled)
  • 1 cup condensed milk (sweetened)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts or chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream until soft peaks form.
  2. Add condensed milk and vanilla extract, then gently fold everything together.
  3. Mix in chopped nuts, chocolate chips, or any desired add-ins.
  4. Pour the mixture into an airtight container.
  5. Freeze for at least 6-8 hours or overnight.
  6. Scoop and serve chilled!
